About this document

This document proposes the addition of a TEI module for correspondence. It is the product of a Correspondence SIG task force (Sabine Seifert, Marcel Illetschko, Peter Stadler) with valuable input from various people.

The aim of the proposal is to define a common set of correspondence-specific meta data, brought together at one place in the TEI header. The lack of special guidelines for encoding letters, postcards, etc. with TEI P5 has been a recurring topic since well before the formation of the Correspondence SIG in 2008. Additionally, there is a growing demand of correspondence projects for linked data facilities. While some of this correspondence-specific information can already be encoded in various sections of the TEI header we believe it to be of greater value for projects dealing with correspondence material to have this information in one place. For the encoding, we suggest two approaches within this set:

  • an elaborate structure for detailed data capture,
  • a basic structure for easy data interchange (CIF).

A guiding idea has been to only tentatively modify the TEI standard by merely adding a few elements, wrapping them in a special purpose element <ct:correspDesc> and injecting the latter into <sourceDesc>. Please note that <ct:correspDesc> is not intended to and can not replace the whole TEI header.

These efforts are supposed to be just a first step in the Correspondence SIG's strive for a complete model of encoding correspondence material.

Conventions used

As required for TEI conformance, non-TEI elements are defined in a distinct non-TEI namespace. In the usage examples and throughout this document that namespace is mapped to the prefix ct: (for ‘correspondence task force’), while TEI elements are not marked by any namespace prefix.

1. Preceding work

The development of <ct:correspDesc> was inspired mostly but not exclusively by the work of DALF as well as the WeGA.

The DALF project, the Digital Archive of Letters by Flemish Authors and Composers from the 19th & 20th century, was launched by the Belgian Centre for Scholarly Editing and Document Studies (Centrum voor Teksteditie en Bronnenstudie) and is one of the best documented projects in the area of correspondence encoding. In 2003, specific DTDs and guidelines for the description and encoding of modern correspondence material, both intended for use with TEI P4, were published.[1] The practical realization as well as the main features for encoding meta data are discussed in [2]. In August 2013 the DALF guidelines were reworked for the new requirements in TEI P5 as a ‘Preliminary P5 Proposal’; that version served as an important inspiration for this proposal.

Before DALF updated its customization from P4 to P5, the Carl-Maria-von-Weber-Gesamtausgabe (WeGA, Carl Maria von Weber – Collected Works) had developed project-specific guidelines for encoding correspondence material in TEI P5[3] and published recommendations in June 2011.

Both projects define a special wrapper element for correspondence meta data while the depth of its structure and its child elements differ slightly. In each case this wrapper element (<dalf:letDesc> and <wega:correspDesc>, respectively) is part of the <sourceDesc> element. DALF introduces two child elements: first, <dalf:letHeading> in which they encode the author and addressee of the letter as well as place and date when it was written; and second, <dalf:letDesc> which contains additional information, e.g. the formal classification of the letter, or an indication of the presence of an envelope or illustrations. The WEGA, however, groups this information directly within the wrapper element: sender1 and addressee, the place where and the date when the letter was not only written but also received, incipit and position within the thread of correspondence (korrespondenzstelle). Both projects decided to leave all other information on providence, physical appearance, history, etc. in <msDesc> where there are already sufficient TEI elements with which to encode them.

In this proposal, we mainly considered the overlapping of these two projects’ encoding guidelines and merged them into one.

2. Theoretical Considerations

In this document correspondence is regarded as an act of communication, in which the message of a sender reaches—mediated and time-delayed—an addressee at a different place. Such an act of communication becomes manifest in a piece of correspondence, that may be tangible “object”, characterised by its “materiality” and co-determining the communication, and that is generally related to a medium or process of delivery. Hence, correspondence has a hybrid nature between event and object.

As an act of communication is directed at someone, each piece of correspondence has one or more addressees, who may or may not be individually identifiable. Therefore, the scope of “correspondence” in this proposal covers not only letters, postcards, telegrams, and similar “material” pieces of correspondence but also electronic communications, e.g. e-mails and text messages.

2.1. What is correspondence-specific information (in terms of the TEI)?

An act of communication has a sender of a message (<ct:sender>), who is not necessarily identical to the author of the text of the message.

An act of communication generally has no unknown audience/unspecified recipient but one or more (intended, perhaps fictional) addressees (<ct:addressee>), “spoken” to by the piece of correspondence.

A piece of correspondence generally has a certain time or period of writing and a definable time of sending—even though it may be unknown or indeterminate (<ct:dateSender>). Similarly, a piece of correspondence was generally written at a particular location(s) (the place of writing), although that location may be unknown; and was most often sent from a particular location (the place from which sent), which may or may not be the same as the place of writing. Very often these bits of information are evident in an address line, postmark, electronically generated sending metadata, etc. In any case, they may be recorded in the <ct:placeSencer>.

A piece of correspondence generally has a process or a medium of transfer (messenger, carrier, postman, carriage, fax machine, internet, etc.) (<ct:transmission>).

Very often a particular message is sent in order to trigger an answer (in form of one or more pieces of correspondence). A single piece of correspondence therefore is normally not a secluded entity but a (written) act of communication in a communication continuum[4], in which it is defined by its relative position between messages sent “before” and “after” (<ct:context>).

Finally, correspondence may be categorized in terms of its object- and/or eventlike nature (e.g. letter, signed personally; telegram; copy; draft) requiring a separate element <ct:correspClass>.

2.2. And what is left out?

Information concerning the manuscript description, e.g. (postage) stamps, seals, water marks, form fields, letter heads, and incipits, are not considered part of the proposed correspondence description. These aspects are not necessarily correspondence-specific but rather characteristic for manuscripts in general and are thus best encoded within <msDesc>.

As this proposal deals with the meta data of correspondence, aspects relating to correspondence-specific transcriptional encoding within <text>, e.g. with <postscript>, <opener>, <closer>, or <signed>, are not considered.

3. Implementation of the correspondence description

The <ct:correspDesc> element provides a detailed description of correspondence-specific metadata. The <ct:correspDesc> element focuses on the communicative function of correspondence, i.e. sender, addressee, and transmission. It is provided as a supplement to the physical description of a correspondence object which is to be encoded within the <sourceDesc>. The description is organized by the following elements

  • sender contains the name or some other description of the sender of a piece of correspondence
  • addressee contains the name or some other description of the addressee of a piece of correspondence
  • placeSender (place of sender) contains the place where the piece of correspondence was written or sent
  • placeAddressee (place of addressee) contains the place of the addressee or the place where the piece of correspondence was actually received
  • dateSender (date of writing/sending) contains the date of writing or sending a piece of correspondence
  • dateAddressee (delivery date) contains the date of receiving or reading a piece of correspondence
  • transmission describes the method and transmitter of the piece of correspondence
  • context (context) provides references to preceding or following correspondence related to this piece of correspondence
  • correspClass (correspondence classification) describes the piece of correspondence (e.g. letter, postcard, email), if possible according to some (external) classification scheme
    schemeidentifies the controlled vocabulary within which the set of keywords concerned is defined.

These elements are all members of a model class called model.correspDescPart; additional features may be defined by defining new elements and adding them to that class.

A simple example might be encoded as follows:
<ct:correspDesc    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <ct:sender>Adelbert von Chamisso</ct:sender>  <ct:addressee>Louis de La Foye</ct:addressee>  <ct:placeSender>Vertus</ct:placeSender>  <ct:placeAddressee>Caen</ct:placeAddressee>  <ct:dateSender when="1807-01-29">29 January 1807</ct:dateSender>  <ct:correspClass>   <term>lettersheet</term>   <term>signed personally</term></ct:correspClass>  <ct:context>   <ref     type="prev"     target="http://tei.ibi.hu-berlin.de/berliner-intellektuelle/manuscript?Brief023ChamissoandeLaFoye#1">Previous letter of Adelbert von Chamisso to Louis de La Foye:      16 January 1807</ref>   <ref     type="next"     target="http://tei.ibi.hu-berlin.de/berliner-intellektuelle/manuscript?Brief025ChamissoandeLaFoye#1">Next letter of Adelbert von Chamisso to Louis de La Foye: 07      May 1810</ref></ct:context>  <ct:transmission>   <p>Post</p></ct:transmission></ct:correspDesc>
By default, order and number of the above elements are not constraint facilitating the encoding of several entities of each feature provided that they all belong to the same communication process. Hence, it is possible to differentiate between the intended and the actual (place of) addressee with the type attribute. In other cases, the respective elements are simply repeated. See for example the postcard of the gathered philologists in Prague to their colleague Seuffert in Graz on the occasion of the first informal ‘Germanistenabend’ which features several <ct:sender> elements with one entry each:
<ct:correspDesc    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <ct:sender>Adolf Hauffen</ct:sender>  <ct:sender>Hans Lambel</ct:sender>  <ct:sender>Hans Meyer</ct:sender>  <ct:sender>Richard Rosenbaum</ct:sender>   <!-- … -->  <ct:addressee>Bernhard Seuffert</ct:addressee>  <ct:placeSender>Prag</ct:placeSender>  <ct:placeAddressee>Graz</ct:placeAddressee>  <ct:dateSender when="1898-01-22">22.01.1898</ct:dateSender></ct:correspDesc>

3.1. Actors, places and dates of correspondence

The very basic information about correspondence consists of the participating actors, their respective locations and dates (of writing/sending and reception). While there is a semantice difference between the sender of a message and the author of the corresponding text, in most cases those two will be identical. To reduce the redundancy of markup and error-proneness, standard linking mechanisms can be applied to connect the <author> element within <titleStmt> to the appropriate <ct:sender> element.
<fileDesc    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <titleStmt>   <title>Letter of Weber to his wife. Dresden, January 1, 1817</title>   <author xml:id="author1">Carl Maria von Weber</author>  </titleStmt> <!-- … -->  <sourceDesc>   <ct:correspDesc>    <ct:sender sameAs="#author1"/> <!-- … --></ct:correspDesc>  </sourceDesc> </fileDesc>
In other cases (cf. the example of collaborative authorship above), there might as well be a clear distinction of the intellectual author of the text and the sender of the message, be it in disguise or frankly. Examples include pre-formulated greeting cards, forced dictated messages, or copied love letters.
The <ct:placeSender> and <ct:placeAddressee> elements are provided to capture the names of spatial locations of sender and addressee respectivly. It is possible to differentiate those places further with the type attribute, e.g. to distinguish the intended place of the addressee (as noted on the address) from the actual place of reception; or to capture several places of writing when the letter was produced over several days at several places. Where applicable, these names can be encoded in more detail with elements provided by the namesdates module described in 13 Names, Dates, People, and Places. If an (external) placeography is present or authority files are available it is also highly recommended to identify those places via unique URIs.
<ct:correspDesc    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">   <!-- … -->  <ct:placeSender ref="http://www.geonames.org/3067696">   <settlement>Prag</settlement></ct:placeSender>  <ct:placeAddressee ref="http://www.geonames.org/2778067">   <settlement>Graz</settlement></ct:placeAddressee> <!-- … --></ct:correspDesc>
As with other information compiled within <ct:correspDesc>, the spatial information is supposed to be editorial, normalised data, not necessarily the exact reproduction of the original address which should rather be encoded within <text>. However, it is possible to provide more address-like detail:
<ct:correspDesc    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">   <!-- … -->  <ct:placeAddressee>   <ab>    <address>     <orgName type="Company">SubMarine Telegraph Company</orgName>     <street>58 Threadneedle Street</street>     <settlement>London</settlement>     <postCode>EC</postCode>    </address>   </ab></ct:placeAddressee> <!-- … --></ct:correspDesc>

3.2. Transmission of correspondence

Because a piece of correspondence generally has a process/a medium of transfer the <ct:transmission> element allows to clearly identify e.g. messenger (Pheidippides who orally delivers the news of the victory against the Persians from Marathon to Athens), carrier (person who takes Goethe's letters to Charlotte Schiller), postman (official letter carrier), carriage, fax machine etc. The <ct:transmission> element therefor in some respect is related to channel in the 15.2.1 Text Description (for example regarding the technical channel ‘internet’ for emails), but it may also refer to persons involved in the process of communication/transmission as well as to the method of transmission without assigning responsibility to a person, organisation etc. <ct:transmission> can both be used as an empty element with according type (and subtype)Note: I'd prefer an attribute for the purpose, rather than type. attributes or in a more verbose way with child element(s).

<ct:transmission    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <p>Übermittelt per Post auf dem Landweg</p></ct:transmission>

3.3. Context and classification of correspondence

The exact position of a piece of correspondence as an act of communication within a communication continuum is a defining feature. This position between a preceding and a following message is encoded within <ct:context>. The evalution of the exact position, the Korrespondenzstelle, might differ between projects due to constraints of (un)available material or different concepts, e.g. all known letters between person A and B across several repositories vs just the letters from person A to person B of one collection in one repository. For the encoding of the previous and following pieces of correspondence, <ref> or <ptr> elements may be used with pointing attributes or <p> for a more general description, as follows:
<ct:context    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <ptr    type="next"    subtype="toAuthor"    target="http://tei.ibi.hu-berlin.de/berliner-intellektuelle/manuscript?Brief101VarnhagenanBoeckh"/>  <ptr    type="prev"    subtype="fromAuthor"    target="http://tei.ibi.hu-berlin.de/berliner-intellektuelle/manuscript?Brief103BoeckhanVarnhagen"/></ct:context>
<ct:context    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <ref    target="http://weber-gesamtausgabe.de/A040962">Previous letter of Carl Maria von Weber to Caroline Brandt: December 30, 1816</ref>  <ref    target="http://weber-gesamtausgabe.de/A041003">Next letter of Carl Maria von Weber to Caroline Brandt: January 5, 1817</ref></ct:context>
The classification of correspondence (according to external or project specific schemes) can be achieved by employing the standard mechanisms of text classification as described in 2.4.3 The Text Classification. However, it might be desirable to capture the object- and/or eventlike nature of correspondence in situ and to merge the textual and contextual information with data about the physical appearance. For this purpose the <ct:correspClass> element provides a simple means to collect these features:
<ct:correspClass    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <term>picture postcard</term>  <term>signed personally</term>  <term>without envelope</term></ct:correspClass>
The proper place for the detailed description of the ‘materiality’ of the correspondence object(s) and possible attachments is still the <sourceDesc> element (and probably <msDesc>), nevertheless it is possible to extract simple oppositions to facilitate processing of this information (e.g. 'without envelope'). These subject terms can either be according to a private scheme or by reference to some external classification system as used in library and documentation work in which case the scheme attribute should identify the according system by its URI.
<correspClass   scheme="http://d-nb.info/standards/elementset/gnd#SubjectHeadingSensoStricto">  <term ref="http://d-nb.info/gnd/4008240-4">Brief</term>  <term ref="http://d-nb.info/gnd/4140367-8">Unterschrift</term> </correspClass>

4. Correspondence Interchange Format (CIF)

Next to encoding with an elaborate structure for detailed data capture, there is the possibility to use the basic structure of the ‘Correspondence Interchange Format (CIF)’. It is designed to enable easy data interchange between digital editions and projects and is used in combination with established standards, e.g. authorial files for persons and places, ISO-codes for dates, etc.

5. Future Work

After dealing with correspondence-specific meta data, the next steps are:

  • restructuring correspondence-related transcriptional elements, as there are several problems with elements like <postscript>, <opener>, <closer>, etc.
  • developing best practice recommendations, with examples, for the encoding of correspondence material.

Namespacehttp://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc
Modulecorrespondence
Member of
model.correspDescPart
Contained by
correspondence: correspDesc
May containCharacter data only
Declaration
element sender { text | ( ( model.nameLike.agent | model.pLike+ ), model.noteLike? ) }
Example
<ct:sender    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">Weber, Carl Maria von</ct:sender>
Example
<ct:sender    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <persName ref="http://d-nb.info/gnd/118520040">Adelbert von    Chamisso</persName></ct:sender>
NoteIn the simplest form the <ct:sender> element consists of text only. By inclusion of the namesdates module described in 13 Names, Dates, People, and Places it is possible to encode the names in more detail.

Appendix B.1.10 <transmission>

<transmission> describes the method and transmitter of the piece of correspondence
Namespacehttp://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc
Modulecorrespondence
Member of
model.correspDescPart
Contained by
correspondence: correspDesc
May containEmpty element
Declaration
element transmission { ( ( model.nameLike.agent | model.pLike+ ), model.noteLike? )? }
Example
<ct:transmission    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc">  <persName ref="https://viaf.org/viaf/46172473/">Pheidippides</persName></ct:transmission>
Example
<ct:transmission type="internet"    xmlns:ct="http://wiki.tei-c.org/index.php/SIG:Correspondence/task-force-correspDesc"/>
Note<ct:transmission> can both be used as an empty element with according type (and subtype) attributes or in a more verbose way with child element.
